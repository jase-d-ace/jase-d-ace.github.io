About

Who Am I?

I used to be a part of corporate America, pushing spreadsheets for an investment firm. After realizing that a life like that wasn't for me, I made my part-time hobby into a career choice and took up the mantle of Full Stack Web Developer. General Assembly gave me the hard skills I need to be a developer, and NYU ingrained in me all of the skills I need to work well in a team. As someone who is driven by results, I always approach a problem with a never-say-die mindset, and I'm always ready to work with my colleagues to tackle any issue that is bigger than me.

Jason Says

A new take on an old classic; Jason Says is an interactive game that does its best to trip you up. It takes the simplicity of Simon Says and adds a fun level of competition where the computer is trying its hardest to make you lose.

  • Client: General Assembly WDI
  • Date: February 2017
  • Techs: HTML5, CSS3, jQuery

Magic: the Info Gathering

Any nerd knows that Magic: the Gathering is an expensive hobby. This app allows you to search for any Magic card and build a deck with them. Now, you don't need to spend thousands of dollars picking up your singular copy of Black Lotus; you can just look at the card here!

  • Client: General Assembly WDI
  • Date: March 2017
  • Techs: HTML, jQuery, CSS, Express, Passport

Bison Bookshelf

Bison Bookshelf is a social media book-reading app done in collaboration with my colleague. This app allows you to save all of your favorite books and review them. You can also follow other readers and see all of the books they review.

  • Client: General Assembly WDI
  • Date: April 2017
  • Techs: Ruby, Rails, Devise

Pokémon Safari Zone

Avid Pokémon fans will remember the Safari Zone as the only place where you could catch certain types of rare Pokémon. This rendition of it simulates that experience, in that you can go buy equipment to begin waiting for Pokémon. Once one has found you, you'll have the opportunity to try to catch it.

  • Client: General Assembly WDI
  • Date: April 2017
  • Techs: React.js, Express, Passport, Socket.io

Dungeons and Dragons Initiative Tracker!

A small app that I built for my friend (and Dungeon Master) for our Dungeons and Dragons group that allows us to fluidly move through a battle, with initiative tracking functionality that gives our DM full control over the participants of a battle.

  • Client: Personal Development
  • Date: March 2018
  • Techs: Node.js, React.js, Socket.io, Webpack, PostgreSQL
